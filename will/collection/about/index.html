time-lapse satellite
about

Time lapse satellite is a website that allows the user to navigate select locations gathered using Google Earth to watch and experience the change that they undergo over time using the historical imagery tool. I find several aspects of this project interesting: first, its visually satisfying to watch the patterns of landscapes change, whether it’s sand dunes in a desert or traffic in a city. Second, the visual, graphic nature of the images are beautiful. Finally, it is amazing how accessible technology can record history via satellite. I am interested in using Google Earth to travel through time to observe how industrialization and natural changes have altered the planet’s surface. Through distant satellite imagery I have seperated the users’ positive and negative feelings towards change and have made them more objective and interested in the images for art's sake.

William Sumrall created this website while attending the introduction to web design course taught by Jerel Johnson (assisted by Michael McDermott), at the Rhode Island School of Design in August 2014.
