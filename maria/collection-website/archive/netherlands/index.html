Netherlands

Think of The Netherlands, the minute pearl of Europe, tucked away between such giants as Germany and France. Yet The Netherlands, while small in size, are grandiose in their history, their culture, and their art. When thinking of Holland, you may think of its lively capital city Amsterdam, of its fields of beautiful tulips, human-made canals and incredible dams, and its lovely windmills.

Yet Netherlands is not only known for its colorful clogs, flowers as well as the royal color orange, but more importantly for its enormous contribution to the world of art. Some of the largest giants of art were born in this minute country, so while you track through their charming birthplace, do pay them a homage by stopping by here:

In Amsterdam: Hermitage Museum, Van Gogh Museum, Stedelijk Museum, Rijksmuseum

Elsewhere: Frans Hals Museum in Haarlem, Vermeer Centrum in Delft
