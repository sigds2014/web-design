synaesthesia

Kandinsky likened synaesthesia to a string instrument, a note struck on one string causing the others to vibrate.

01. Interrelated sensations
Kandinsky maintained his belief in the synaesthetic phenomenon, and he included relationships of sensations in his teaching notes.
“The tactile feeling aroused by yellow is hard, resistant, sharp, and prickly, while blue is soft, unresistant, and velvety. In smell, the contrast is piquant versus aromatic, and in hearing, sharp and penetrating versus low and self-contained.”
—Wassily Kandinsky
02. Sixth sense
Kandinsky also listed qualities involving a sixth sense, kinesthesia, the internal awareness of bodily movements and tensions.
“Kandinsky connected yellow with broken, jerky, and short movements and blue with movements that are rounded and slow. He added a diagram indicating the relationship between yellow and angular lines and blue and rounded ones.”
—Clark V. Poling
