simultaneous contrast

Kandinsky used small squares or circles on contrasting backgrounds to demonstrate that they appear as dissimilar as possible.

01. Hue and lightness
Two contiguous colors will appear as dissimilar as possible in both their hue and lightness.
“When yellow and red are juxtaposed, the yellow will look greenish, and the red will look purplish. If the two colors are complementaries, they are intensified by being placed next to each other.”
—Clark V. Poling
02. Boundary effects
Along the edges of the color the complementary of the neighboring hue seems to disappear.
“As Kandinsky saw it, red appeared on both sides of the boundary of yellow and blue, that is, the two colors were tinged respectively orange and purple.”
—Clark V. Poling
