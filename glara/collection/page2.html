Spatial Phenomena

Kandinsky designated two types of spatial phenomena: changes in apparent spatial position and size of colored shapes.

01. Spatial Position
Spatial position can be demonstrated by drawing a blue and yellow circle of the same size.
“The yellow streams outward, moves away from the center, and approaches almost visibly toward the spectator. The blue, however, develops a centripetal movement, and withdraws from the spectator.”
—Wassily Kandinsky
02. Size of colored shapes
The yellow shape leaps over its boundaries, appearing larger than the blue.
“A dark object appears smaller than a bright one of the same size, [whereas a luminous image seems to expand, and even] acts as energy beyond itself; the dark object spreads from the center to the periphery.”
—Johann Wolfgang von Goethe
