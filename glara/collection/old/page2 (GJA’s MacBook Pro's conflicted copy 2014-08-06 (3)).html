Spatial Phenomena

Kandinsky designated two types of spatial phenomena: changes in apparent spatial position and size of colored shapes.

01. Spatial Position
Spatial position can be demonstrated by drawing a blue and yellow circle of the same size. "If one makes two circles of the same size and fills one with yellow and the other with blue, one notices after only a short period of concentrating upon these circles that the yellow streams outward, moves away from the center, and approaches almost visibly toward the spectator. The blue, however, develops a centripetal movement, and withdraws from the spectator"
—Wassily Kandinsky
