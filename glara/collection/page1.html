polarity of pairs

The concept of polarity, the plus and minus of colored pairs, constitutes the core of Kandinsky’s color theory.

01. yellow and blue
Kandinsky designated yellow and blue as the fundamental pair to study, comparing them to white and black in their opposition.
“Yellow is the color nearest the light. It appears on the slightest mitigation of light…always carries with it the nature of brightness…and blue always brings something of darkness with it…it flees and draws us after it, [providing] a feeling of cold.”
—Wassily Kandinsky
02. Temperature and Value
Kandinsky assigned exercises of stepped gradations to demonstrate the “two great divisions” of color: temperature and value. The above example shows a cool red on the left and a warm red on the right, each progressing in its value.
“For every color there are four main sounds: warm, and either light or dark; or cold, and either light or dark.”
—Wassily Kandinsky
